PROCEDURE

Mixing area

Cotton mixing, also known as cotton blending, is a crucial process in the textile industry that involves combining different types of cotton fibers to achieve desired qualities in the final product. This practice has been employed for centuries and serves multiple purposes in yarn manufacturing.

Carding

Carding is a critical process in the spinning of cotton and other fibers, serving as the second stage in yarn manufacturing. This process transforms loose, unorganized fibers into a uniform strand known as sliver, which is essential for subsequent spinning operations.

Simplex or Roving Frame

The doffing process in a spinning mill is critical for maintaining productivity and efficiency in yarn production. This automated system involves the removal of full packages of yarn from spinning machines and replacing them with empty ones, significantly reducing downtime and labor costs.

Ring Frame

The ring frame process is a crucial stage in yarn manufacturing, primarily used to convert roving into finished yarn. This process involves three main actions: drafting, twisting, and winding

AutoConer

The Autoconer is a crucial machine in the spinning process, primarily used for winding yarn into larger packages while ensuring high efficiency and quality. Its operation integrates several advanced technologies to enhance productivity and minimize defects in the yarn.
